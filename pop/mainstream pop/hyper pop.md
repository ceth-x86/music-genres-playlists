# Hyper Pop

## Wave 1 — PC Music & foundations (2013–2016)

**Dux Content — Lifestyle (Lifestyle, 2013)**
A formative PC Music-era cut where glossy synth-pop tropes are refracted through aggressively artificial textures. Its plastic-bright timbres and deadpan futurism foreshadow hyperpop's 'too-much' aesthetic.

**QT — Hey QT (Hey QT (Single), 2014)**
The fictional-pop concept meets hyperreal sound design: candy-coated hooks rendered with high-gloss, synthetic precision. It helped define hyperpop's fascination with branding, avatars, and pop-as-art-object.

**LIZ — When I Rule the World (When I Rule the World (Single), 2014)**
A bubblegum-bass blueprint built on pitched vocals and toybox synths that push pop sweetness into uncanny territory. Its maximal polish and hyper-stylized affect became core hyperpop markers.

**A.G. Cook — Beautiful (PC Music Volume 1, 2015)**
A defining PC Music statement: bright, videogame-like synth work and exaggerated pop melodicism. The track's 'kawaii' sheen and audacious artificiality became foundational to later hyperpop aesthetics.

**Hannah Diamond — Every Night (PC Music Volume 1, 2015)**
An early hyperpop 'perfect pop' simulation—angelic vocals and glossy, over-compressed production that feels almost inhuman. It set the template for hyperpop's uncanny sincerity and synthetic intimacy.

**Lipgloss Twins — Wannabe (PC Music Volume 1, 2015)**
A maximalist rewrite of teen-pop signifiers, sped up and sharpened into a cartoon rush. Its sparkling, plastic percussion and bright hooks exemplify bubblegum-bass as hyperpop's first wave.

**Thy Slaughter — Bronze (PC Music Volume 1, 2015)**
A surreal, high-saturation club-pop hybrid where synthetic textures are foregrounded as the 'lead instrument.' It shows early hyperpop's interest in texture as spectacle as much as melody.

**EASYFUN — Fanta (PC Music Volume 1, 2015)**
Effervescent and hyper-detailed, turning a simple pop rush into a compressed, high-sugar sonic product. Its bright, buoyant production language became a cornerstone of the PC Music sound.

**Clarence Clarity — Those Who Can't, Cheat (No Now, 2015)**
A dense, glitchy pop construction that pushes hooks through fractured, hyperactive sound design. It's a key bridge between experimental pop and the later hyperpop maximalist palette.

**Madonna — Bitch I'm Madonna (Rebel Heart, 2015)**
Mainstream pop filtered through hyperpop's plastic clatter and extreme sound design, making a major star sound gleefully synthetic and abrasive. It signaled how hyperpop aesthetics could infiltrate radio-scale pop.

**Brooke Candy — XXXTC (XXXTC (Single), 2015)**
A brash, hyper-gloss club track that spotlights the genre's love of high-impact synthetic percussion and cartoonish vocal processing. It helped connect early hyperpop production to rap and club energy.

**Danny L Harle — Super Natural (PC Music Volume 2, 2016)**
A turbocharged, neon trance-pop take that amplifies pop euphoria to absurd intensity. The track's supersized synths and gleaming compression are textbook first-wave hyperpop.

**GFOTY — Poison (PC Music Volume 2, 2016)**
Weaponizes bubblegum hooks with glitchy, metallic edges and deliberately 'cheap' digital textures. It captures hyperpop's camp relationship to taste, irony, and synthetic pleasure.

**Felicita — a new family (PC Music Volume 2, 2016)**
Bright, hyperactive synthesis and abrupt, playful structure shifts make pop feel like a collage of animated GIFs. It exemplifies the genre's post-internet sensibility and texture-forward songwriting.

**Life Sim — IDL (PC Music Volume 2, 2016)**
A glassy, melancholic variant of the PC Music sound—still hyperreal, but emotionally hazed. It shows how hyperpop can render vulnerability through artificial surfaces.

**Charli XCX — Vroom Vroom (Vroom Vroom EP, 2016)**
A watershed moment where pop's club form is rebuilt with industrial gloss and hyper-stylized vocal attitude. It effectively popularized hyperpop's 'futuristic banger' template for a wider audience.

## Wave 2 — Metallic hyperpop & crossover breakthroughs (2017–2020)

**Vince Staples — Yeah Right (Big Fish Theory, 2017)**
A pivotal moment where hyperpop-grade sound design enters rap at full volume, with abrasive textures and synthetic bounce. It helped normalize 'SOPHIE-style' metallic percussion and maximal polish in hip-hop-adjacent pop-rap spaces.

**SOPHIE — Immaterial (Oil of Every Pearl's Un-Insides, 2018)**
An archetypal hyperpop manifesto: ecstatic melody, pitch-play, and hyperreal sound design that makes pop feel like a new physics. It's widely treated as a defining statement of the genre's utopian, shape-shifting identity.

**Bladee — Be Nice 2 Me (Icedancer, 2018)**
Drains pop of 'human' affect and replaces it with glassy hooks and weightless vocal processing, anticipating later digicore's emotional distance. Its internet-native sheen helped connect cloud-rap aesthetics to hyperpop's synthetic pop language.

**COBRAH — U Know Me (U Know Me (Single), 2018)**
A club-forward hyperpop cut that's sleek yet aggressively compressed, with stylized vocals and a fetishized synthetic punch. It shows how the genre can be both pop-hooky and nightlife-hard at once.

**Iglooghost — Mametchi / Usohachi (Steel Mogu, 2018)**
A whirlwind of cartoon sound design, chiptune-like brightness, and abrupt style switches—hyperpop as animated collage. It's influential in demonstrating how maximalist detail can still support strong pop momentum.

**Shygirl — Uckers (Uckers (Single), 2019)**
Minimalist but menacing: hyperpop's synthetic scream-samples and sharp-edged sound design applied to club-rap swagger. It helped establish the UK 'Nuxxe' lane where hyperpop meets deconstructed club.

**Slayyyter — Mine (Mine (Single), 2019)**
Takes Y2K pop gloss and renders it hyperreal, with bright compression and exaggerated hooks that flirt with parody while staying undeniably catchy. It represents the 'silky' end of hyperpop: pop maximalism without losing sing-along clarity.

**Dorian Electra — Flamboyant (Flamboyant, 2019)**
A theatrical, gender-bending pop spectacle with blown-out synths and deliberate camp excess. It captures hyperpop's affinity for performance art, persona-play, and maximalist production as identity construction.

**Quay Dash — U.A.F.W.M. (U.A.F.W.M. (Single), 2019)**
A fierce, synthetic club-rap hybrid that emphasizes hyperpop's high-impact drum design and processed vocal attitude. It's a key example of how the genre's sound can amplify rap bravado without becoming standard trap.

**Ecco2K — Peroxide (E, 2019)**
Featherlight melodies and heavily treated vocals float over glossy, digital textures—hyperpop's tenderness rendered through artifice. It's influential in the 'drain/digi' corridor where pop hooks and internet minimalism meet.

**Mechatok — Not Okay (Not Okay (Single), 2019)**
Bright, squeaky synth design and plasticized rhythm programming push club-pop into a hyperreal, toy-like space. It helped define a European strain of hyperpop that's sleek, ironic, and texture-obsessed.

**Eartheater — Supersoaker (Trinity, 2019)**
Hyperpop-adjacent experimental pop that treats the voice and beat as malleable plastic, bending club energy into surreal form. Its glossy abrasiveness and body-focused groove align with hyperpop's sound-design-first ethos.

**Kim Petras — 1,2,3 dayz up (Turn Off the Light, Vol. 2, 2019)**
Hyperpop at its most hedonistic: candy hooks colliding with exaggerated, metallic production flourishes. It shows how the genre can turn straight-up pop songwriting into something uncanny via sound-design extremity.

**Jazmin Bean — Yandere (Yandere (Single), 2019)**
A hyperpop-meets-emo cut where cute/violent aesthetics are baked into both sound and persona. Its pitch-play and compressed, candy-toxic production helped define the 'scene' side of late-2010s hyperpop.

**Sega Bodega — Salv Goes to Hollywood (Salvador, 2020)**
A cinematic, warped-pop track that uses extreme processing and sudden tonal shifts to make emotion feel synthetic and huge. It's emblematic of the producer-led wing of hyperpop, where sound design is narrative.

**COUCOU CHLOE — Joyride (Joyride (Single), 2020)**
A sleek, clubby mutation with icy textures and hyper-detailed percussion that keeps pop melody at arm's length. It represents a darker, fashion-forward lane adjacent to UK hyperpop's deconstructed club crossover.

**Arca — KLK (KiCK i, 2020)**
Latin rhythms reframed through alien sound design and maximal digital distortion—hyperpop's genre-collision principle at global scale. It broadened the idea of hyperpop beyond Anglo pop into avant-club futurism.

**Rico Nasty — IPHONE (Nightmare Vacation, 2020)**
A blown-out, pitch-pushed banger that fuses rap energy with hyperpop's abrasive compression and synthetic squeal. It's a key example of hyperpop's crossover power in mainstream-leaning rap spaces.

**Namasenda — Dare (AM) (PC Music Volume 3, 2020)**
Turns Eurodance immediacy into hyperpop intensity: shiny synth stabs, high-pressure compression, and stylized vocal sheen. It's a modern PC Music lineage track that connects early bubblegum-bass to the Spotify-era boom.

**Cecile Believe — Made in Heaven (Plucking a Cherry from the Void, 2020)**
A crystalline vocal performance framed by hyperreal electronic detail—emotional pop rendered as high-gloss digital sculpture. As a key voice in the PC Music orbit, she helped define hyperpop's 'angelic but artificial' affect.

## Wave 3 — Gecs-era maximalism & core hyperpop anthems (2019–2021)

**100 gecs — money machine (1000 gecs, 2019)**
The viral flashpoint that crystallized hyperpop's second wave: blown-out bass, pitch-warped vocals, and genre-smashing swagger. It made maximalist 'internet pop' feel like a new mainstream possibility.

**food house — 8 now (food house, 2020)**
A full-speed collage of trance stabs, clipped vocals, and hyperactive rhythm edits—hyperpop as ADHD dance music. It became a cult favorite for how ruthlessly it compresses pop euphoria into chaotic structure.

**fraxiom — GMFU (GMFU (Single), 2020)**
A frantic, meme-ready banger where pitch-shifted vocals and explosive drums turn heartbreak into a digital tantrum. It captures the DIY, internet-first energy that fueled hyperpop's platform-era spread.

**gupi — Thos Moser (Thos Moser (Single), 2020)**
A classic 'glitch-pop' hyperpop cut: glossy synths, abrupt edits, and a hook that feels like a corrupted ringtone. It's influential for marrying melodic sweetness to jittery, hyper-detailed production.

**Tony Velour — Do or Die (Do or Die (Single), 2020)**
A rap-meets-hyperpop gateway track where stadium-sized synths and clipped vocals collide with hip-hop cadence. It's emblematic of the Dylan Brady-adjacent lane that made hyperpop feel immediately banger-ready.

**ElyOtto — SugarCrash! (SugarCrash! (Single), 2020)**
A TikTok-era breakout that distilled hyperpop's bright compression, pitchy hooks, and 'too-cute' melodrama into a compact pop sugar rush. Its virality helped bring the tag to a mass audience.

**SEBii — BAFFLEdd (BAFFLEdd (Single), 2020)**
Bubblegum melody strapped to trap speed and glitched percussion, embodying the 'digicore' edge within hyperpop. It's a defining example of youth-scene energy translated into hyperpop maximalism.

**Boofbby — online_v3!!! *BOUNCE BACK* (online_v3!!! *BOUNCE BACK* (Single), 2020)**
Pixel-cute vocals and hyperactive drums make the track feel like an arcade game rendered as pop. It shows hyperpop's obsession with internet texture—nostalgia, speed, and synthetic brightness.

**osquinn — i dont want that many friends in the first place (i dont want that many friends in the first place (Single), 2020)**
A tender, glitched pop confession framed by pitch-shifted vocals and sparkling distortion. It's influential in the digicore corridor for turning bedroom feelings into hyperreal digital pop.

**Alice Gas — Ferrari (Ferrari (Single), 2020)**
Hard-edged, hyper-synthetic club-pop with aggressive compression and abrasive sparkle. It represents the harsher, more industrial-leaning pocket of the gecs-era underground.

**That Kid — Taco Bell (Taco Bell (Single), 2020)**
Peak 'scene' hyperpop: diaristic lyrics and candy hooks delivered at breakneck speed with glossy, distorted production. It helped define the pop-punk-inflected side of the movement's online youth culture.

**Chase Icon — Srs (Srs (Single), 2020)**
A campy, high-gloss banger that weaponizes pop diva attitude through hyperpop's synthetic punch. Its over-the-top persona-play is central to hyperpop's performance-first ethos.

**Yameii Online — Baby My Phone (Baby My Phone (Single), 2020)**
A virtual-pop avatar track that leans into hyperpop's identity-play and 'post-internet' character building. The bright, plastic sonics and animated cadence make it a textbook internet-era hyperpop artifact.

**Petal Supply — Party Till We Die (Party Till We Die (Single), 2020)**
An ecstatic, ravey take on hyperpop where chiptune brightness and trance momentum meet pop hooks. Petal Supply's work helped codify the 'cute but loud' production grammar in the underground.

**Ravenna Golden — Boy Blue (Boy Blue (Single), 2020)**
A high-drama pop cut where sugary melody is pushed through compressed, maximal sound design. It's a standout for merging classic pop songwriting with the hyperreal textures of the gecs-era wave.

**Laura Les — Haunted (Haunted (Single), 2020)**
A raw, blown-out hyperpop single where pitch-shifted vocals and distortion turn vulnerability into catharsis. It's emblematic of the genre's ability to make 'too much' sound feel emotionally direct.

**Dylan Brady — WTF I'm Crying (WTF I'm Crying (Single), 2020)**
A producer-as-pop-star moment: hyper-detailed drums, abrupt edits, and hook-first songwriting built from internet noise. It spotlights the sound-design maximalism that defined the post-2019 hyperpop wave.

**Deko — Phantasy Star Online (Phantasy Star Online (Single), 2021)**
A pop-punk/emo melody refracted through hyperpop's glitchy sheen and exaggerated energy. It captures the scene's habit of treating genre as a playlist rather than a boundary.

**Cake Pop — Sick Beat (Cake Pop, 2021)**
Dylan Brady's side-project distills hyperpop's maximalist club DNA into dense, hook-stuffed pop structures. It's emblematic of the producer-led wing that treats sound design as the main attraction.

**Voda Fuji — sorry haha i fell asleep (sorry haha i fell asleep (Single), 2021)**
A micro-hit from the digicore ecosystem that turns chat-message intimacy into compressed, glitchy pop. Its style—pitchy vocals, fast drums, and diaristic humor—captures the scene's platform-native songwriting.

**6arelyhuman — Hands Up! (Hands Up! (Single), 2021)**
A modern 'scene' hyperpop staple that channels MySpace-era melodrama through pitch-shifted vocals and blown-out dance drums. It's key for showing how hyperpop reanimates past teen-pop cultures in hyperreal form.

**umru — check1 (check1, 2021)**
Producer-forward hyperpop that treats sound design like lead vocals—air-horns, squeaks, and precision percussion as pop drama. It helped define the 'clubby' side of the gecs-era scene.

## Wave 4 — Digicore / glitchcore / Discord-SoundCloud wave (2020–2022)

**brakence — fwb (punk2, 2020)**
A landmark 'glitch-pop' record where virtuosic guitar songwriting is fused with hyperpop's cut-and-paste editing and vocal processing. It helped prove the scene could sustain real musicianship alongside maximal digital aesthetics.

**d0llywood1 — ihonestlymightjustgiveup (ihonestlymightjustgiveup (Single), 2020)**
A scene-defining digital diary: pitch-shifted vocals, squeaky synths, and emotional immediacy delivered at meme-speed. It's a key artifact of the DIY, platform-native teenage wave.

**blackwinterwells — waste my time (waste my time (Single), 2020)**
Hyperpop reframed as tender bedroom confession, with glossy distortion and clipped, internet-paced phrasing. It represents the more introspective end of the digicore spectrum without losing the genre's synthetic edge.

**asteria — Stressed (Stressed (Single), 2020)**
A high-energy, pop-leaning digicore anthem built from compressed drums and pitch-stacked vocals. It exemplifies how the scene turned teenage anxiety into bright, hyperreal singalongs.

**Kawaii Ry — Moshi Moshi (Moshi Moshi (Single), 2020)**
A hyper-cute, sped-up pop approach that pushes sweetness into uncanny digital excess. It shows the continued influence of nightcore and anime aesthetics inside the digicore ecosystem.

**capoxxo — numb (numb (Single), 2020)**
A raw, emo-coded digicore cut where cracked melody and distortion create cathartic intensity. It's emblematic of how the scene reprocessed pop-punk feelings through hyperpop's digital tools.

**lieu — Like This (Like This (Single), 2020)**
A melodic, pitched-vocal hyperpop track that foregrounds bright synths and snappy, compressed drums. It's a key example of the bedroom-pop to hyperpop pipeline in the early 2020 wave.

**i9bonsai — IMAGINE GETTING (IMAGINE GETTING (Single), 2020)**
An aggressive, internet-brained rap-hyperpop crossover built for short-form impact: blunt hooks, loud drums, and synthetic bite. It's representative of the meme-forward corner of the SoundCloud wave.

**glaive — astrid (All Dogs Go to Heaven, 2021)**
One of the signature teen-wave anthems—breakneck drums, emo melody, and heavily processed vocals that feel like an internet scream. It helped define 'digicore' as the emotionally raw, hyper-fast wing of hyperpop.

**ericdoa — movinglikeazombie (COA, 2021)**
A sleek, hooky entry point where pop-rap cadence meets hyperpop's glitched sheen and stacked vocal processing. It captures how the scene professionalized its sound while keeping its internet-born urgency.

**midwxst — trying (trying (Single), 2021)**
A rap-forward digicore cut that still foregrounds hyperpop's exaggerated vocals and hyper-detailed percussion. It's important for showing how the teen-wave merged trap energy with maximal pop sound design.

**aldn — glittr (glittr (Single), 2021)**
Emo songwriting and big pop hooks rendered in neon: distorted guitars, pitched vocals, and explosive drops. Aldn became a key figure in turning 'scene' nostalgia into hyperpop's modern form.

**8485 — 1:15 (Plague Town, 2021)**
A crystalline, introspective take that uses hyperpop's processing to magnify intimacy rather than aggression. It's influential for the softer, more atmospheric side of the 2021 wave.

**underscores — Spoiled little brat (fishmonger, 2021)**
A brilliantly written pop song disguised as chaotic collage, mixing punk attitude with hyperpop's sound-design maximalism. It's central to the genre's 'anything goes' ethos while still landing huge hooks.

**Jane Remover — movies for guys (frailty, 2021)**
Hyperpop emotional realism: diaristic lyrics framed by glitched drums, pitch-play, and sudden stylistic pivots. It's a cornerstone of the post-gecs teen wave and its move toward fuller album statements.

**Rebecca Black — Crumbs (Crumbs (Single), 2021)**
A late-career reinvention that embraces hyperpop's metallic gloss and exaggerated hooks to reframe a pop-culture narrative. It's notable for translating hyperpop aesthetics into a clean, radio-ready pop structure without losing edge.

**Lil Mariko — Don't Touch (Don't Touch (Single), 2021)**
Hyperpop's abrasive side: shrieked vocals, distorted synths, and meme energy pushed to cartoon violence. It helped popularize the 'gremlin' lane where shock, humor, and catharsis converge.

**Sewerslvt — Pretty Cvnt (Skitzofrenia Simulation, 2021)**
A hard, speed-driven track that brings rave intensity and internet overload aesthetics into the hyperpop orbit. It highlights the scene's openness to hardcore tempos and abrasive digital texture.

**Hayd — Suffocate (Suffocate (Single), 2021)**
A hooky, emo-leaning hyperpop cut that balances clean melody with glitchy processing and big, compressed drops. It represents the wave of pop vocalists adopting hyperpop's production grammar to heighten emotional punch.

**XO WILLOW — GOOD ENOUGH (GOOD ENOUGH (Single), 2021)**
A metallic, pop-punk-flavored track where hyperpop's synthetic sheen amplifies youthful melodrama. It's important as an example of hyperpop functioning like a new 'radio rock' for online scenes.

**Stomach Book — Lament (Lament (Single), 2021)**
A glitchcore-adjacent track that treats vocals as malleable texture—stuttered, stretched, and reassembled. It represents the experimental edge of the teen wave, where sound design becomes emotional language.

**oaf1 — Think Abt It (Think Abt It (Single), 2021)**
A digicore staple where pop hooks collide with compressed trap percussion and digital distortion. It captures the scene's ability to make rap structure feel 'hyper' without losing melodic focus.

**Glitch Gum — H3LL0 K1TTY (H3LL0 K1TTY (Single), 2021)**
A cartoonishly bright, ravey hyperpop cut that leans into internet iconography and high-speed hooks. It captures the scene's knack for turning meme aesthetics into real pop propulsion.

**kurtains — fml (fml (Single), 2021)**
A diaristic, deadpan track that uses minimal hooks and glitchy production to make emotional exhaustion feel digital. It represents the wave's move toward understated, internet-intimate songwriting within hyperpop's sonic palette.

**dazegxd — Falling for You (Falling for You (Single), 2021)**
A pop-punk-leaning hyperpop track that pushes emo melodicism through bright, synthetic compression and glitchy drops. It reflects how hyperpop became the default production language for a new online 'scene' rock.

## Wave 5 — Post-boom diversification & newer hybrids (2022–2025)

**Doss — Strawberry (Strawberry (Single), 2016)**
A glittery club-pop track that emphasizes hyperpop's love of glossy synth design and high-pressure compression. It's influential in the lane where hyperpop overlaps with rave and trance textures.

**kets4eki — krush (krush (Single), 2022)**
A hard-edged, speed-driven take that leans into hyperpop's distorted brightness and frenetic rhythm design. It represents the rise of micro-scenes that blend hyperpop with club substyles and online aesthetics.

**Astra King — Baby (Baby (Single), 2022)**
A glossy, diaristic hyperpop single where vocal processing and synthetic sparkle heighten emotional immediacy. It captures the continuing DIY spirit—bedroom-pop feelings rendered in hyperreal textures.

**tsubi club — laced up (laced up (Single), 2022)**
A pop-forward hyperpop cut that fuses scene melodrama with clean, loud digital production. It's representative of the wave of artists translating online youth culture into big, melodic hyperpop choruses.

**Pinkii — DONT WANNA SLEEP (DONT WANNA SLEEP (Single), 2022)**
Fast, candy-bright hyperpop that leans into chiptune color and clipped, pitchy vocals. It represents the micro-scene approach: short, intense tracks optimized for shareability and mood.

**Babymorocco — GHOSTT (GHOSTT (Single), 2022)**
A dark, club-leaning hyperpop track where synthetic percussion and warped vocals create fashion-forward menace. It shows the post-boom drift toward sleeker, nightlife-oriented hyperpop forms.

**BANSHEE — KILL ALL PREDATORS (KILL ALL PREDATORS (Single), 2022)**
A heavier, cathartic hybrid where hyperpop's vocal processing and maximal drums meet alt/rock intensity. It reflects the post-boom trend of folding hyperpop production into aggressive guitar-forward forms.

**Frost Children — FLATLINE (Speed Run, 2023)**
A post-boom hyperpop track that keeps the maximalist punch but channels it through crisp dance structures and pop hooks. It shows how the genre matured into cleaner, more 'club-functional' songwriting while staying hyperreal.

**2hollis — crush (crush (Single), 2023)**
A post-boom example of hyperpop absorbing rap cadence and sleek electronic texture into concise, high-impact songwriting. It reflects the genre's shift toward tight, hook-first tracks with still-hyperreal sound design.

**Odetari — Narcissistic Personality Disorder (Narcissistic Personality Disorder (Single), 2023)**
A late-wave 'internet banger' built around hyperpop's clipped vocal attitude and high-energy, synthetic drum programming. It shows the genre's continued evolution toward club-ready, meme-friendly hooks.

**SOPHIE POWERS — U Love It (U Love It (Single), 2023)**
A modern, pop-leaning hyperpop track that uses bright compression, pitch-play, and attitude as the hook. It shows how hyperpop's production toolkit became a new mainstream 'edge' aesthetic for rising artists.

**Oklou — God's Chariots (The Rite of May, 2018)**
An ethereal, glossy take where hyperpop's artificial textures are used to create intimacy and dreamlike space. It represents the softer 'angelic' lane—maximal detail without maximal aggression.

**Kero Kero Bonito — The Princess and the Clock (Civilisation II, 2021)**
A bright, fast, synth-saturated pop cut that embraces hyperpop's exaggerated momentum and sparkling artificiality. It's a great example of how indie-pop acts adopted hyperpop's hyperreal production grammar.

**TAAHLIAH — T-9 (TAAHLIAH, 2019)**
A club-forward UK hyperpop track where sharp synth design and heavy processing create futuristic pop attitude. It represents the producer-led lane that bridges hyperpop with experimental club.

**LSDXOXO — Sick Bitch (Dedicated 2 Disrespect, 2021)**
A hard, nightlife-focused hybrid that shares hyperpop's abrasive sound design and hyperreal vocal treatment. It shows the genre's ongoing dialogue with club forms and deconstructed dance energy.

**Himera — Cyan Hardcore (Cyan Hardcore (Single), 2020)**
A rave-inflected hyperpop cut that channels trance/hardcore euphoria through modern, hyper-detailed production. It highlights the post-boom drift toward faster tempos and club-functional maximalism.

**Alice Longyu Gao — Rich Bitch Juice (High Dragon and Universe, 2021)**
Hyperpop's internet-theatre side: bold persona, sharp sound design, and punchline hooks delivered with hyperreal polish. It highlights how the genre thrives on character, attitude, and producer-forward texture.
