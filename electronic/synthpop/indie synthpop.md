# Indie Synthpop

## Foundational Indie Synthpop (2000–2004)

**The Postal Service — Such Great Heights (Give Up, 2003)**
One of the core 'laptop-pop' anthems that made indie synthpop feel emotionally diaristic rather than purely dance-oriented. Its chiming synth arpeggios and plainspoken vocal delivery became a template for countless indie-electronic crossover bands.

**The Knife — Heartbeats (Deep Cuts, 2003)**
A defining indie synthpop ballad where the drum-machine pulse supports (rather than replaces) classic pop melody. Its spare arrangement and bittersweet hook showed how minimalist synth palettes could still land like a big chorus.

**Ladytron — Seventeen (604, 2001)**
A blueprint-era indie synthpop cut where Ladytron fuses minimal drum-machine pulse with pop-forward songwriting. It helped set the genre's early balance between DIY electronic texture and immediately memorable hooks.

**Frou Frou — Let Go (Details, 2002)**
A blueprint-era indie synthpop cut where Frou Frou fuses synth-led chorus lift with pop-forward songwriting. It helped set the genre's early balance between DIY electronic texture and immediately memorable hooks.

**The Sounds — Living in America (Living in America, 2002)**
A blueprint-era indie synthpop cut where The Sounds fuses icy electro-pop timbres with pop-forward songwriting. It helped set the genre's early balance between DIY electronic texture and immediately memorable hooks.

**Junior Senior — Move Your Feet (D-D-Don't Don't Stop the Beat, 2002)**
